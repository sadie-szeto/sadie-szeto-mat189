About Sadie's Research

Sadie Szeto is diving deep into the evolving trends of cameras, particularly those from the early 2000s, which are making a notable resurgence. With the rise of nostalgic aesthetics, she’s observed that celebrities are now bringing back the camcorder, a beloved device that once captured life’s most treasured moments in a grainy, raw, and authentic way. This follows the recent revival of digital cameras, signaling a broader trend where older technology is being re-embraced for its unique charm and the distinct visual experiences it offers.

In her analysis, Sadie explores how these retro tools are becoming cultural symbols once again, driven by a collective longing for a simpler, more tangible form of memory-making. Through her research and insights, she highlights the cyclical nature of trends and the power of nostalgia in shaping contemporary culture. This investigation is part of her ongoing exploration of how past technologies influence present-day media and fashion.
